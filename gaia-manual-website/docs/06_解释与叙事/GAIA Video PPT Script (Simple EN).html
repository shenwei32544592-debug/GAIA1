GAIA — Video PPT Script

Simple English | For Video / Pitch Deck

Purpose: Consolidated script and content source for GAIA video or PPT. Use each section as slide or scene.

Slide 1 — Title

GAIA
Global Art & Identity Alliance

The ideal order for artists — achievable.

Slide 2 — What Does the Alliance Want to Do?

Build the ideal order for artists:

  • Creative sovereignty respected
  • Attribution preserved — source cannot be erased
  • Revenue flows back to creators — 65% to artists
  • Peer consensus decides disputes — no single authority

Implementation path: protocol freeze → physical artifacts → external validation → platform launch → multi-node expansion.

Slide 3 — Why Does the Alliance Exist?

Current systems fail artists:

Problem Reality
Attribution lost Creators disappear from history and revenue while their work is copied and commercialized
Justice controlled by power Who counts as "original" is decided by platforms, lawyers, capital — not peers
Dignity reduced to data Art becomes files and pixels, little real-world presence

No global protocol today protects creator sovereignty, immutably records attribution, routes revenue to sources, and uses peer consensus for disputes.

GAIA exists to establish that protocol.
A civilization-level set of rules, not a single company or platform.

Slide 4 — Five-Step Path

Step Phase What Happens
① Protocol freeze Rules fixed, printed, not arbitrarily changed
② Physical occurrence Real artifacts made, signed, sealed
③ External confirmation At least one external party signs, joins, endorses
④ Platform live Orders accepted, money received, splits executed
⑤ Multi-node expansion More artists, more clients; system self-sustaining

Each step is verifiable and auditable. No layer skipping.

Slide 6 — Hard Prerequisites for Real-World Achievement

To achieve this goal in the real world, three hard conditions are required:

  1. Legal — Agreements frozen, enforceable, compliant
  2. IT — Platform, allocation engine, traceability in place
  3. Writing — Rules and protocols documented, transparent, persistent

Slide 7 — Why This Is Achievable

The hardest preconditions are already in place.

  • Legal ✓ — Charter and Carrier Protocol can be frozen; legal core is signable
  • IT ✓ — Architecture, allocation engine, traceability designed and in place
  • Writing ✓ — Rules and archives documented; printable, citeable

This is not wishful thinking.
It is achievable.

Slide 8 — Closing

GAIA — the ideal order for artists.

Creative sovereignty. Attribution preserved. Revenue to source. Consensus over power.

Hard prerequisites met.
Not empty hope.
Achievable.
